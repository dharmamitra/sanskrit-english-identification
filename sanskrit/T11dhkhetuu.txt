Explanatory Remarks
A number of five figures at the left side indicates the location of the key word
in the basic texts In the case of the SV
the HB and the VN
the first three figures
indicate the page number
and the last two indicate the line number for example
 In the case of the
PV and the NB the second figure indicates the chapter number and the last three
figures indicate the karika or sutra number for example PVPV the
Pramanasiddhichapter v NBNB
the Pararthanumanachapter sutra 
In the case of the SP
the last two figures indicate the karika number for example
 In this database
sentences are artificially divided into wordunits
although they
involve a phonetic fusion or union in the basic texts Further
compound words are
divided into their shortest constitutive elements we use a hyphen to indicate that
hyphenated elements originally form a compound word
with the following
exceptions
 The prefixes a
dur
nih remain undivided
irrespective of attachment to
words or compound words
 Suffixes making an adverb
for example vat
vasa
are divided But suffixes
making a possessive adjective
for example vat
mat
remain undivided and terms
such as a numeral dha
vidha
prakara remain also undivided
 Compound words with the suffixes ta
tva or bhava
bhuta fundamentally
remain undivided For example
karyakaranabhavabhutata
 Compound words with evam
tat
tatha
para
yatha
su sva in the
wordhead fundamentally remain undivided For example
evamjatiya
tadutpatti
tathakrta paratantra
yathartha
yathasvam
sujnanatva svabhava
 Some compound words which are regarded as terminology remain also
undivided For example agnihotra

viruddhavyabhicarin

 Those terms whose wordhead is subject to the Sandhi rules for example d
dhetu are cited in their original form for example d hetu

using this database to make an KWIC index

 paraupalakṣaṇatvād yady api jñānaṃ siddham
tathā api tāv eva
 bhāvaabhāvau kecid darśanaadarśanamātreṇa vyavasthāpayanti iti
 tanniṣedhaartho niścitaśabda uktaḥ
sator api bhāvaabhāvayor
 anvayavyatirekayoḥ sattāyāṃ saṃśayāt tasmād yataḥ sattā
 prasādhakapramāṇād anayor niścayas tadadhīnā iti jñāpanaartham
 asmābhir grahaṇaṃ kṛtam
 yato api bhāvaabhāvavacanamātreṇa tatsādhanapramāṇaākṣepasiddhiḥ
 pṛthag ato jñānaṃ lakṣaṇaṃ na bhavati
tena eva arthasya avagatatvād
 upanayaarthavat pakṣadharmatvāt anvayavyatirekayor
 api tarhi na pṛthaktvam
ekasya prayogād ubhayagater
 iti cet na hetoḥ sapakṣaasapakṣayor bhāvaabhāvayor aparaspara
 ākṣepāt ekaṃ vākyam ubhayaṃ gamayati ity ucyate
 na eko artho dvitīyasya nanu tatra eva bhāvo tadabhāve
 ca abhāva iti vākye parasparaākṣepa iti cet
vacanam etad ekasya
 api niyamakhyāpakasya dvitīyaākṣepanāntarīyakatvāt sāmarthyād
 ubhayam ākṣipati na punaḥ kevalau bhāvaabhāvāv ākṣipataḥ
 niyamavantau ca na kevalau
niyamasya ubhayarūpatvāt
 tasmāt tatra eva bhāva iti na bhāva eva ucyate
itareṇa api na abhāva
 eva yena bhāvo abhāvo vā dvitīyam ākṣipet na evaṃ jñānaṃ
 paraupalakṣaṇāt trailakṣaṇyād vyatirekitam iti na lakṣaṇaantaram
 tasmān na hetuḥ ṣaḍlakṣaṇaḥ
 hetubindur ācāryadharmakīrtikṛtaḥ samāptaḥ
